Chapati Recipe

Chapati served on a plate.

A staple in Kenyan households, Chapati, also known as "Chapo," is a versatile flatbread that complements a variety of dishes. Made from a simple yet skillfully crafted dough, this unleavened bread offers a delightful combination of softness and slight crispness. Whether served with curries, stews, or enjoyed on its own, Chapati's widespread popularity speaks to its status as a beloved and essential component of Kenyan cuisine.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 and Â½ cups warm water (adjust as needed)
  • Additional flour for dusting
  • Extra oil or butter for frying

Steps

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Make a well in the center.
  2. Add the vegetable oil and gradually pour in the warm water while mixing with a spoon or your hands to form a soft, non-sticky dough.
  3. Knead the dough on a floured surface for about 5-7 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic.
  4. Divide the dough into 8-10 equal portions and roll each portion into a ball.
  5. On a floured surface, roll out each ball of dough into a thin, round circle using a rolling pin.
  6. Brush a thin layer of oil or butter over the surface of the rolled-out dough.
  7. Starting from one end, fold the dough into a narrow strip, then coil it into a round shape like a snail, tucking the end underneath.
  8. Flatten the coiled dough with your palm, then roll it out again into a thin circle.
  9. Heat a non-stick pan or a griddle over medium heat and place the rolled-out chapati on it.
  10. Cook for about 1-2 minutes until small bubbles start to form, then flip it over and cook the other side for another 1-2 minutes until golden brown spots appear.
  11. Remove from the pan and repeat the process with the remaining dough balls.
  12. Serve the delicious Kenyan Chapatis (Chapos) hot with your favorite curry, stew, or any side dish.
