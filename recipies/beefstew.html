Beef Stew Recipe

Beef Stew in a bowl

A hearty and flavorsome delight, Kenyan Beef Stewis a culinary marvel that reflects the country's rich heritage. With tender chunks of beef enveloped in a fragrant blend of local spices and vegetables, this stew embodies the warmth and diversity of Kenyan cuisine. Often enjoyed with a side of rice, chapati, or ugali, this dish has solidified its place as a beloved comfort food among Kenyan households.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg beef, cubed
  • 3 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 3 tomatoes, chopped
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, minced
  • 2 cups beef or vegetable broth
  • 2 potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 2 carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro or parsley for garnish (optional)

Steps

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the onions and saut√© until they turn soft and translucent.
  2. Add the minced garlic and ginger, and cook for another minute until fragrant.
  3. Toss in the beef cubes and brown them on all sides.
  4. Stir in the chopped tomatoes and tomato paste, and cook until they soften and start to break down.
  5. Pour in the beef or vegetable broth and bring the mixture to a gentle simmer.
  6. Add the potatoes, carrots, bell pepper, Worcestershire sauce, paprika, curry powder, and bay leaf. Season with salt and pepper.
  7. Cover the pot and let the stew simmer for 1.5 to 2 hours, or until the beef is tender and the vegetables are cooked.
  8. Adjust the seasoning if needed and remove the bay leaf before serving.
  9. Garnish with fresh cilantro or parsley if desired.
  10. Serve the Kenyan Beef Stew hot, preferably with rice, chapati, or ugali.
